Challenges & Solutions

Developing an interactive, physics-based dice rolling application demanded cutting-edge animations and 3D rendering capabilities natively within Flutter.

Realistic Physics Simulation

Standard animations looked fake; the dice needed to bounce and roll realistically.

Integrated a 2D/3D physics engine directly into Flutter via FFI to calculate gravity, collision, and momentum accurately.

High Frame Rate Rendering

Rendering 3D dice meshes caused frame drops below 60FPS on older hardware.

Utilized low-polygon models and hardware-accelerated canvas drawing to ensure silky-smooth 120FPS animations on supported displays.

Haptic Feedback Integration

Users couldn't 'feel' the dice hitting the virtual table.

Engineered precise, pattern-based haptic feedback that triggers exact vibrations at the exact millisecond of physics collisions.

State Synchronization

Multiplayer mode required all users to see the exact same dice roll outcome.

Moved the random number generation and physics seed to the server, ensuring deterministic rendering across all devices.
